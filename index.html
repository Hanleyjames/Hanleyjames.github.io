A little about James

Hello, I am a Software Engineer located north of Seattle. Originally I studied and passed an IT program in Highschool in Florida. I was trained in CompTIA A+ certification. For my senior year project, I created maps for the old Half-Life 2 Source Engine. I took a break from the tech field and pursued some other interest of mine. In college, I pursued a PoliSci and Philosophy degree. I studied at the University of Central Florida. After a long discussion with a few professors and teachers (who told me that I would do best if I got two Ph.D.'s), I decided to go back to working in the tech field.

This leads me to where I am today! After studying and working with Ruby on Rails and doing Lab Tech work at Microsoft, I decided to take it seriously. I worked with Epicodus and started an internship with DTOOR, where I helped with Front End development and deployment. After that, I worked as a part-time T.A. for the University of Washington with Trillogy. There I assisted with their MERN stack Bootcamp program. Afterward, I worked with PTG and helped with internal and external projects. This gave me a lot of experience in working with multiple clients and their unique demands. Each problem comes with a different stack and solution. Each opportunity has been an amazing chance for me to learn. During my time as a Software Engineer, I have worked with startups, large enterprises, and currently at a co-op part time. The wonderful experience of working at a co-op is the horizontal team organization and communication. The benefit of these experiences is that I have learned how to deal with many different teams of all sizes.

Portfolio

  • Lynx Dumper

    This is a small chrome extension that connects to a Nodejs app. It takes a url and email, sends it to a Node.js service, then runs lynx as a console command in a docker container running ubuntu. The service then uses a mailer with Nodejs to send you the webpage back in email form, scrubbing it of ads and javascript. This is more of just a goofy fun side project. I built it after a concept Richard Stalman made at MIT. The source code and privacy policy are linked in the extension as the home page.
